Has your work been published?

Publication means distribution of copies to the public by sale, rental, lease, or lending. Offering to distribute copies to a group for further distribution also constitutes publication.

Important: Public performance or display does not necessarily constitute publication. Online posting may or may not be considered publication depending on the circumstances.

Deposit Requirements

The deposit requirement varies based on your work type and publication status:

General Requirements:
  • Unpublished works: One complete copy or phonorecord
  • Published works: Two complete copies of the best edition
  • Works published only outside the US: One complete copy as first published

Electronic vs. Physical Deposits:

  • Many works can be uploaded electronically through the eCO system
  • Some works require physical deposits to be mailed
  • Best edition requirements apply to published works

Filing Fees

Copyright registration fees vary depending on the type of registration and filing method:

Standard Online Registration Fees (as of 2024):
  • Single author, same claimant, one work: $45
  • Standard application: $65
  • Paper filing: $125
  • Group registrations: Vary by type ($65-$95)

Payment Methods:

  • Credit or debit card (online)
  • Electronic check (online)
  • Copyright Office deposit account

Determining If Your Work Is Published

Under copyright law, "publication" is the distribution of copies of a work to the public by sale or other transfer of ownership, or by rental, lease, or lending.

Examples of Publication:

  • Selling books, CDs, or DVDs to the public
  • Distributing copies of software online for download
  • Posting content online with no restrictions on copying or sharing
  • Distributing copies to a group for purposes of further distribution

NOT Considered Publication:

  • Performing a play or showing a movie to an audience
  • Displaying artwork in a gallery
  • Broadcasting a television program
  • Posting content online with restrictions on copying (e.g., behind a login)
Still not sure? When in doubt, it's often safer to treat your work as unpublished for registration purposes. You can also consult with the Copyright Office or a legal professional.

üîå Registration via API: For High-Volume Registrants

The U.S. Copyright Office provides a Registration API for qualified high-volume registrants who need to submit multiple copyright registrations programmatically. This is particularly useful for:

  • Publishing companies registering hundreds of works
  • News organizations registering daily newspapers
  • Photography agencies registering large photo collections
  • Software companies with frequent releases

API Access Requirements

To use the Copyright Office API:
  1. Establish a Copyright Office deposit account (minimum $450 balance)
  2. Request API credentials through the Copyright Office
  3. Receive your personal API key and authentication credentials
  4. Review API documentation and technical specifications
  5. Implement API integration in your system

API Capabilities

The Registration API allows you to:

  • Submit Applications: Programmatically create and submit registration applications
  • Upload Deposits: Upload digital deposit files directly
  • Track Status: Query the status of pending applications
  • Retrieve Certificates: Download registration certificates when issued
  • Manage Fees: Automatically deduct fees from your deposit account
